When it rains I know I have had enough.
Enough of the sodden feet, enough of the mouldering buildings, enough of the rustling palms, enough of the gulls calling my name. Enough.
The city is dying too. Too many people, too many places to bury my head.
I have run laps around this body. I have sneaked past the hip joints, swimming upstream to the knee.
I have been up all night thinking about death. What if I didn't wake up until day?
Wouldn't that just be another reason to die?
